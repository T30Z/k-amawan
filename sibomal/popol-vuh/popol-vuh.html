THE POPOL WUJ

    The Popol WUJ

    The Book of the People




    Introduction

    The *Popol Vuh*, often referred to as the "Book of the People," is a foundational text of the Quiché Maya, one of the most significant indigenous groups of Mesoamerica. Written in the K'iche' language, the *Popol Vuh* is a rich tapestry of mythology, history, and cultural tradition, documenting the cosmological beliefs, heroic narratives, and genealogical histories of the Quiché people.

    This translation project aims to bring the *Popol Vuh* to a wider audience, faithfully preserving the essence and nuances of the original K'iche' text while making it accessible to contemporary readers. The translation is structured into four parts, reflecting the natural divisions within the original manuscript:

    • Part I: The Creation of the World and the Gods
    • Part II: The Adventures of the Hero Twins
    • Part III: The Genealogy of the Quiché People
    • Part IV: The Migration, Settlement, and Struggles of the Quiché

    The *Popol Vuh* serves as both a religious text and a historical record, providing invaluable insights into the worldview of the Quiché Maya. Its stories of creation, gods, and heroes are deeply intertwined with the cultural identity and spiritual beliefs of the Maya people.

    This translation is based on the K'iche' text as preserved in various manuscripts, most notably the Dresden Codex. Care has been taken to remain as close to the original text as possible, while also ensuring that the translation is readable and engaging for modern audiences.

    The *Popol Vuh* continues to be a subject of academic study and cultural preservation, recognized for its literary significance and its role in understanding the pre-Columbian civilizations of the Americas. By providing this translation, we hope to contribute to the ongoing appreciation and study of this monumental work.

    Acknowledgments are due to the scholars, translators, and indigenous communities who have preserved and passed down the *Popol Vuh* through generations. Their dedication ensures that this vital piece of cultural heritage remains accessible to all.

    PART I: The Creation of the World and the Gods

    Chapter 1: The Beginning of the Ancient Word

    THIS IS THE BEGINNING OF THE ANCIENT WORD
    Here we shall write,
    we shall set down the ancient word,
    the narrative,
    the beginning and the origin of all things done in the town of K'iche'.

And here we shall recount:
    we shall bring it to light,
    because it was hidden,
    because it was lost,
        the account,
        the declaration,
        the prayer,
        the invocation of the Creator,
            the Maker,
            the Mother and Father of life,
                of all beings,
                the Giver of Breath,
                the Giver of Heart,
                the Bearer,
                the Sustainer,
                the Protector,
                the Guardian,
                the Creator,
                the Maker,
                    Tepew,
                    Q'ukumatz,
                    the Heart of the Sky,
                    the Heart of the Earth,
                    the Mighty Mountain,
                    the Great Earthquake.
Here is the account,
    the beginning and the origin,
    of all that was done in the town of K'iche'.

    Chapter 2: The Creation of the Earth

    AND THEN THE EARTH WAS SHAPED
    The waters were separated, and the land appeared.
        Mountains rose, and valleys sank.
        The waters were divided, and the land was formed.
        The mountains stood high, and the valleys lay low.
        The plains spread out, and the forests grew.
        The rivers and streams flowed, filling the earth with life.
    And the Creator and Maker said: "Let there be light."
        And there was light, and the sky was brightened.
    The sun and the moon were placed in the sky.
    The stars were set in their places to guide the way.

    Chapter 3: The Creation of Life

    AND THEN THE CREATION OF LIFE BEGAN
    The birds were created, and they filled the sky with their songs.
    The animals were created, and they roamed the earth.
    The fish were created, and they swam in the waters.
    The plants were created, and they grew across the land.
    The Creator and Maker saw that it was good,
        but they desired more.
        They desired beings who could speak,
            who could worship and praise them.

    Chapter 4: The First Attempts at Creation

    AND THEN THEY ATTEMPTED TO CREATE BEINGS WHO COULD SPEAK
    They formed beings out of mud,
        but they were weak and crumbled.
    They formed beings out of wood,
        but they were stiff and empty.
    The beings of wood multiplied,
        but they had no hearts and no minds.
    They could not remember their Makers.
        They were hollow and walked without purpose.
    The beings of wood were destroyed,
        swept away by the waters,
        and their faces were crushed by the flood.

    Chapter 5: The Creation of the First Humans

    AND THEN THE FIRST HUMANS WERE CREATED
    The Creator and Maker decided to use maize,
        the sacred food, to create beings who could worship and sustain them.
    They formed the flesh of the first humans from maize.
    The first humans were four in number:
        Balam-Quitzé,
        Balam-Acab,
        Mahucutah,
        Iqui-Balam.
    These were the names of the first fathers,
        the first humans created by the gods.
    They were strong and wise,
        they knew everything,
        and they saw everything.
    But the gods feared their power,
        so they clouded their vision,
            reducing their knowledge.
    Thus, the first humans came into being,
        and they praised their Makers.

    Chapter 6: The Gift of Knowledge

    AND THEN THE GIFT OF KNOWLEDGE WAS GIVEN
    The Creator and Maker granted the first humans knowledge,
        but only enough to sustain them.
    They could see the world, but not beyond it.
    They could understand the earth, but not the heavens.
    They could remember their Makers and give thanks.
    The gods were satisfied,
    and the first humans lived in harmony with the earth.

    Chapter 7: The Establishment of the Nations

    AND THEN THE NATIONS WERE ESTABLISHED
    After the creation of the first humans,
        their descendants began to multiply.
    The tribes and nations spread across the land,
        each establishing its own identity and customs.
    They built cities, established laws, and created societies.
    The gods watched over them, guiding their progress and ensuring their prosperity.
    The people honored their creators with rituals and offerings,
        never forgetting their origins and the sacred nature of maize.
    The nations grew strong, and their influence spread across the earth.
    But with power came responsibility, and the people were reminded
        of the importance of maintaining balance with the natural world.

    Part II: The Adventures of the Hero Twins

    Chapter 1: The Story of Seven Macaw

    AND THEN SEVEN MACAW REBELLED AGAINST THE GODS
    Seven Macaw, a powerful and prideful bird, declared himself a god.
        He believed he was the sun and the moon, and his arrogance grew.
    The gods were displeased with his vanity,
        and they decided to humble him.
    Hunahpu and Xbalanque, the Hero Twins, were tasked with this mission.
    They set a trap for Seven Macaw, using his greed and pride against him.
    Seven Macaw fell into the trap, losing his teeth and his power.
        He was no longer the shining figure he once was, and his arrogance faded.
    The Hero Twins restored balance to the world, ensuring that true gods were honored.

    Chapter 2: The Defeat of Seven Macaw

    AND THEN SEVEN MACAW WAS DEFEATED
    Hunahpu and Xbalanque, with the help of their grandmother, completed the defeat of Seven Macaw.
    They tricked him into believing he could restore his lost splendor by removing his beak.
    With his beak gone, Seven Macaw was stripped of his power and pride.
        He could no longer deceive the people or challenge the true gods.
    The Hero Twins proved their wisdom and cunning, setting the stage for their future trials.
    The gods were pleased, and they blessed the Hero Twins for their service.

    Chapter 3: The Story of Hunahpu and Xbalanque

    AND THEN THE HERO TWINS BEGAN THEIR JOURNEY
    Hunahpu and Xbalanque were born to a noble family,
        but their father, Hun Hunahpu, had been defeated by the lords of Xibalba.
    The twins were destined to avenge their father’s defeat and restore honor to their lineage.
    They grew up learning the arts of hunting, strategy, and trickery.
    Their grandmother cared for them, and they became skilled in many ways.
    The twins were wise beyond their years, and they understood the nature of the world.
    They set out on a journey to challenge the lords of Xibalba,
        the underworld, where their father had been taken.

    Chapter 4: The Trials in Xibalba

    AND THEN THEY ENTERED THE UNDERWORLD
    Hunahpu and Xbalanque descended into Xibalba, the realm of the dead.
    The lords of Xibalba, who had defeated their father, welcomed them with treachery.
    The twins faced many trials and tests, designed to break their spirit.
    They were sent through houses of darkness, cold, and danger.
        But the twins were clever and resourceful, outwitting the lords at every turn.
    They used their wisdom and magic to survive each trial.
    Finally, they challenged the lords to a ballgame, the same game in which their father had been defeated.
        But this time, the twins knew how to play the game on their terms.

    Chapter 5: The Ascension of Hunahpu and Xbalanque

    AND THEN THE HERO TWINS ASCENDED INTO THE SKY
    After defeating the lords of Xibalba, Hunahpu and Xbalanque were victorious.
    They had avenged their father and restored balance to the world.
    But their journey did not end in the underworld.
    The gods decided to honor their bravery and wisdom by transforming them into celestial bodies.
    Hunahpu became the sun, bringing light to the day.
    Xbalanque became the moon, guiding the night.
    Their journey through the underworld and their victory were immortalized in the heavens,
        and their story was passed down through generations as a testament to their courage and ingenuity.

    Part III: The Genealogy of the Quiché People

    Chapter 1: The Foundation of the Quiché Civilization

    AND THEN THE FOUNDATION OF THE QUICHÉ CIVILIZATION WAS LAID
    The first four humans, Balam-Quitzé, Balam-Acab, Mahucutah, and Iqui-Balam,
        were the ancestors of the Quiché people.
    These were the first fathers, wise and powerful, who guided their descendants.
    They received the teachings of the gods and passed them down to their people.
    Under their leadership, the Quiché nation began to take shape.
    The people built great cities and temples, establishing their presence in the land.
    The gods blessed their efforts, and the nation prospered.
    The descendants of the first fathers multiplied, spreading across the land,
        and their influence grew as they formed alliances with other tribes and peoples.

    Chapter 2: The Lineage of the Kings

    AND THEN THE LINEAGE OF THE KINGS WAS ESTABLISHED
    From the first fathers came the great kings of the Quiché nation.
    Each king was chosen for his wisdom, strength, and ability to lead the people.
    The kings maintained the traditions and laws established by the first fathers,
        ensuring that the gods were honored and the people were protected.
    The Quiché kings were revered for their ability to balance the needs of the people
        with the will of the gods.
    They were warriors and diplomats, securing the nation’s borders
        and fostering peace among neighboring tribes.
    The lineage of kings was a source of pride and continuity for the Quiché people,
        symbolizing the enduring strength and stability of their civilization.

    Chapter 3: The Establishment of the Quiché Kingdom

    AND THEN THE KINGDOM OF QUICHÉ WAS ESTABLISHED
    The kings of the Quiché led their people to greatness, establishing a powerful kingdom.
    They built magnificent cities, with temples that reached toward the sky,
        and their influence extended far beyond their borders.
    The kingdom was marked by its wealth, culture, and devotion to the gods.
    The kings governed with justice, maintaining order and prosperity within the kingdom.
    The people flourished, and the Quiché civilization became known for its achievements
        in art, architecture, and knowledge.
    The kingdom of Quiché stood as a beacon of strength and wisdom,
        a testament to the legacy of the first fathers and the gods who guided them.

    Part IV: The Migration, Settlement, and Struggles of the Quiché

    Chapter 1: The Migration of the Quiché People

    AND THEN THE PEOPLE JOURNEYED TO THEIR NEW LAND
    The Quiché people, under the guidance of their kings and priests,
        set out on a great journey to find a new land.
    They left behind their ancestral homes, carrying with them the sacred knowledge
        and the traditions passed down by the first fathers.
    The journey was long and arduous, filled with challenges and dangers.
    The people faced hunger, harsh weather, and the threat of enemies,
        but they remained steadfast, guided by the prophecies and the will of the gods.
    Finally, they arrived in a fertile and bountiful land,
        where they decided to settle and build their new kingdom.
    This land became the heart of the Quiché civilization, a place where they could thrive
        and continue their legacy.

    Chapter 2: The Kings of Quiché and Their Struggles

    AND THEN THE KINGS OF QUICHÉ FACED CHALLENGES
    As the Quiché kingdom grew, the kings faced many challenges in maintaining their rule.
    Internal conflicts arose among the noble families, threatening the unity of the people.
    There were disputes over land, power, and the interpretation of the sacred laws.
    The kings had to navigate these conflicts with wisdom and strength,
        seeking to preserve the stability of the kingdom.
    They also faced external threats from neighboring tribes who sought to conquer their lands.
    The kings led their armies in battle, defending their people and their territory.
    Despite these struggles, the Quiché kings continued to uphold the traditions of their ancestors,
        ensuring that the people remained faithful to the gods and the teachings of the first fathers.

    Chapter 3: The Arrival of the Spanish

    AND THEN THE SPANISH ARRIVED IN THE LAND OF QUICHÉ
    The arrival of the Spanish marked the beginning of a new and challenging era for the Quiché people.
    The Spanish came with their own customs, beliefs, and ambitions, seeking to conquer the land.
    The Quiché kings and their people were confronted with a powerful and determined enemy.
    The Spanish brought weapons and diseases that the Quiché had never seen before,
        and their numbers were overwhelming.
    Despite their courage and resistance, the Quiché people were eventually overpowered.
    The Spanish imposed their rule, forcing the Quiché to adopt new ways of life.
    The once-great kingdom of Quiché was absorbed into the growing Spanish empire,
        but the memory of their glory and the stories of their ancestors lived on
        in the hearts of the Quiché people.
